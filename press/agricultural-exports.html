Executive Order Signed to Boost Agricultural Exports

New fast-track export process unveiled for coffee and cocoa farmers in southern provinces.

Published March 18, 2026 | Reported by the Ministry of Trade

In a significant move to strengthen the national economy, the Executive Branch has signed a landmark order aimed at streamlining agricultural exports.

The Ministry of Trade today unrolled a new fast-track export process specifically designed for coffee and cocoa farmers in the southern provinces. This initiative is expected to increase international trade volume by 15% within the current fiscal year, providing much-needed support to local producers.

Under the new order, bureaucratic hurdles that have historically delayed the shipment of perishable goods will be significantly reduced. Special "Green Lanes" will be established at major ports to ensure that San Meridian's world-renowned coffee and cocoa can reach global markets while maintaining peak freshness.

"Our farmers are the backbone of our nation," stated a spokesperson for the Ministry of Trade. "By simplifying the export process, we are not only increasing their income but also enhancing San Meridian's reputation as a reliable global supplier of high-quality agricultural products."

The move has been welcomed by agricultural unions, who have long called for reduced red tape. Early projections suggest that the increased trade could inject an additional SMS$450 Million into the southern provincial economies, funding infrastructure improvements and local educational projects.
