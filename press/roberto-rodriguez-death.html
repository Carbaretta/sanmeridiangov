The Mysterious Death of Roberto Rodriguez

Prominent politician and FMC critic found dead in his home. Official cause of death: Heart attack.

Published February 7, 2026 | Reported by the Political Desk

Roberto Rodriguez, a vocal member of the National Assembly and a persistent critic of the Free Meridian Cartel (FMC), was found dead in his Capital District home early this morning.

The official medical report has ruled the cause of death as a sudden heart attack. Rodriguez, 52, had served in the Assembly for over a decade and was widely known for his fearless stance against corruption and organized crime.

However, the timing of his death has raised significant suspicion among the public and his political allies. Rodriguez was reportedly preparing a new series of parliamentary inquiries into the FMC's financial links with local businesses just days before he was found. "Roberto was in excellent health and was more determined than ever," stated a colleague who wished to remain anonymous. "He knew he was being watched, but he refused to be silenced."

The tragedy is compounded by the ongoing search for Rodriguez's 14-year-old daughter, Maria Rodriguez, who went missing several weeks ago. She was last seen playing near the Estudente Community Center in Dos Cervezas. Despite extensive searches, no traces of her have been found, leading many to believe her disappearance was a direct attempt to intimidate her father. In the wake of these events, Roberto's wife, Elena Rodriguez, has reportedly fled the country, citing a profound fear for her own life and the safety of any remaining relatives.

The FMC has denied any involvement in either the death of Rodriguez or the disappearance of his daughter. In a brief statement, a spokesperson for the Estudente Community Center expressed "deep sadness" at the news and offered condolences to the family.

As San Meridian mourns the loss of one of its most courageous voices, the questions surrounding his death continue to mount. Many are now calling for an independent investigation into the circumstances, fearing that the long arm of the cartel has reached into the heart of the nation's political establishment.
