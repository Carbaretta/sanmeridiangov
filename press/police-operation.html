Policia Nacional Confirms Successful Operation in Cartel Crackdown

Chief Juan Law praises tactical units for northern border security and significant seizures.

Published March 11, 2026 | Reported by the Security Desk

The Policia Nacional has confirmed a large-scale tactical operation targeting the Free Meridian Cartel (FMC), resulting in several arrests and the seizure of illegal assets.

Chief Juan Law praised the efforts of the tactical units who secured the northern border this week. The operation resulted in record seizures of illicit substances and the take-down of several key smuggling routes utilized by the FMC to distribute narcotics across the region.

However, behind the celebratory tone of the official statement, internal sources suggest the Policia Nacional is struggling to cope with the increasing pressure. Despite the recent success, the sheer volume of FMC activity has stretched law enforcement resources to their breaking point.

Critics and security analysts have described the crackdown as a "drop in the ocean" in the battle against the country's surging drug activities. While a few routes have been closed, new ones appear to open almost immediately, fueled by the FMC's seemingly endless financial resources and local influence.
