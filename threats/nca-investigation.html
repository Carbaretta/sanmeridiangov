1. Introduction
This document outlines the preliminary findings of a digital forensic examination conducted on a mobile communications device recovered by Merseyside Police during the arrest of the individual identifying as Aimee Anelope on 28th March 2026. The device was submitted to the NCA Digital Analytics Laboratory for deep-layer extraction.
2. Forensic Methodology
Standard forensic protocols were observed to maintain chain of custody. Initial attempts at manual extraction were bypassed due to sophisticated encryption layers. Analysts utilized the Cellebrite Physical Analyzer (v7.58) to perform a physical file system dump. This allowed for the bypass of application-level security and the recovery of deleted and cached data blocks.
3. Key Findings - Telegram Analysis
The examination successfully decrypted local database files associated with the 'Telegram' instant messaging application. Data recovery revealed a history of communications with a high-level distributor or handler. Of particular note is the use of the name "Maria" by the remote sender when addressing the suspect, directly contradicting the identity provided on the recovered San Meridian documentation. The recovered chat logs (see cross-reference below) indicate a highly coordinated narcotics transport operation involving private aviation (Ref: "SMX") and vehicle acquisition in the Speke area.
CROSS-REFERENCE: Detailed chat transcript and forensic metadata can be found in the accompanying automated report generated by the Cellebrite suite.
SERIAL NO: CEL-REP-2026-0402
4. Investigative Recommendations
Based on the recovered communications, it is recommended that the investigation be expanded to include flight manifests for the private terminal at Liverpool John Lennon Airport (SMX) for the 27th and 28th of March. Furthermore, the identification of the recipient as "Maria" supports the Embassy's report that the suspect's current documentation is fraudulent.
